Queues vs Pub/Sub

Queues vs Pub/Sub is a high-value senior interview topic because it reveals whether you understand the underlying mechanism, the default behavior, and the trade-offs in production.

🧒 ELI5

A simple mental picture

Imagine a busy workplace where one small rule determines whether the whole team moves smoothly or keeps stepping on each other. Queues deliver work to consumers, while pub/sub lets one event fan out to multiple interested subscribers. works like that kind of rule: it tells the system how to behave before small mistakes turn into bigger production problems.

It matters because the shape of message delivery should match whether the problem is work distribution or event broadcast. Without that rule, engineers often discover the problem only after the app is under load, when the bug is expensive and confusing.

The useful mental shortcut is to ask: “What is the system trying to coordinate here, and what breaks when that coordination is missing?” That question usually reveals why this feature exists.

🔗 ANALOGY

Why the rule exists

Think of queues vs pub/sub as a piece of operating policy, not just a syntax feature. The policy exists to make decisions repeatable across the whole system instead of relying on every individual class to remember the rule.

If you ignore that policy, the most common smell is using one messaging pattern for every use case without thinking about consumption semantics. In real applications, that smell usually shows up as flaky behavior, hidden coupling, or painful debugging.
